## 1. Purpose

This document establishes the mandatory standard for the creation, structure, governance, versioning, review, issuance, and lifecycle management of all Requests for Quotation (RFQs) within the Meridian programme.

It serves as the foundational grammar for the Meridian ecosystem and ensures consistency, auditability, vendor comparability, AI-graph interoperability, procurement rigour, and long-term maintainability.

---

## 2. Scope

This standard applies to all RFQs issued within the Meridian programme.

### 2.1 Meridian vs Continuum Boundary

Meridian and Continuum are separate programmes.  
This standard governs all Meridian RFQs.  
Continuum RFQs shall follow this standard unless and until superseded by dedicated Continuum governance documentation.  
No Continuum-level RFQ may be issued without an approved Continuum governance charter.

### 2.2 Exclusions

Excluded from this standard: informal discussions, concept exploration, feasibility investigations, and preliminary design brainstorming.

**Procurement Entry Trigger**  
An initiative enters procurement when either:  
- Kevin Slade (Estate Principal) provides written direction to proceed to vendor engagement; or  
- An RFQ identifier is formally assigned.

## 4. Core Principles

### 4.1 Atomic Requirements
All requirements shall express a single concept, be testable, measurable where practical, and avoid bundled assumptions.

### 4.2 Vendor Neutrality
Vendor-neutral language shall be used unless explicitly overridden. Any vendor-specific or brand-specific requirement requires written approval from Kevin Slade and explicit documentation within the RFQ.

### 4.3 Traceability & Graph Alignment
Every RFQ shall maintain requirement traceability, dependency relationships, machine-readable metadata, and AI-graph compatibility.

### 4.4 Repository Authority
The canonical version of every RFQ is the version stored in the Meridian repository. Copies, exports, PDFs, emails, or AI reproductions are informational only.

### 4.5 Human Authority
Human direction takes precedence over AI-generated recommendations. AI systems may draft, analyse, review, score, and recommend. AI systems may not ratify, approve, issue, or authorise procurement. Final authority remains with Kevin Slade or formally delegated human approvers.

## 5. Requirements Structure

### 5.1 Priority Taxonomy

| Priority | Definition |
|----------|------------|
| **P0**   | Mandatory. Non-compliance disqualifies the proposal. |
| **P1**   | Critical. Deviation requires justification and approval. |
| **P2**   | Important. Deviation permitted with explanation. |
| **P3**   | Desirable. Aspirational requirement. |

Every requirement must include one priority classification.

### 5.2 Requirement Identifier Standard
Every requirement shall possess a unique identifier in the format `DOMAIN-###` (e.g. `HVAC-001`, `NET-014`).

### 5.3 Requirement Block Structure
Every requirement shall contain at minimum: `requirement_id`, `priority`, `description`, `verification_method`, and `acceptance_criteria`.

## 10. Versioning, Change Control & Issuance

### 10.1 Status Lifecycle
`draft` → `review` → `issued` → `amended` → `superseded`

### 10.2 Issuance Protocol
An RFQ becomes immutable only upon issuance, which requires:
- Completion of required review stages
- Explicit ratification by Kevin Slade
- Final version number and status `issued`
- Publication to the canonical Meridian repository with signed changelog entry

### 10.3 Amendments
Any amendment requires a new version, change log update, and impact assessment on dependent documents.
