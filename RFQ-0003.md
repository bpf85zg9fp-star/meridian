# RFQ-0003: Meridian Evaluation Rubric & Scoring Standard

**Version 1.3 — Governance-Ready Release (L3 Consolidated)**

---

## 1. Purpose

This document defines a deterministic, auditable, and fully machine-interpretable scoring model for vendor evaluation under the Meridian RFQ framework. It eliminates ambiguity, removes category overlap, enforces confidence-weighted scoring, and establishes comprehensive calibration anchors.

---

## 2. Scope

Applies to all Meridian procurement evaluations, including L2A/L2B automated scoring pipelines and human moderation layers.

---

## 3. Evaluation Architecture

| Category                  | Weight | Definition |
|---------------------------|--------|------------|
| Technical Compliance      | 35%    | Conformance to explicit RFQ requirements (P0/P1/P2 mapped) |
| Cost                      | 30%    | Total cost of ownership (TCO), lifecycle cost, and financial exposure |
| Delivery & Experience     | 15%    | Proven execution capability, comparable history, and timeline realism |
| Risk & Compliance         | 10%    | Regulatory adherence, operational risk depth, and contractual exposure |
| Innovation (Uplift)       | 10%    | Optional only. Extensibility *beyond* baseline specifications |

**Innovation Constraint:** Innovation scoring applies **only** to capabilities that are **not listed** in the RFQ-0000 requirement register. Where interpretive uncertainty exists, the default is to exclude the item from Innovation scoring. **Innovation cannot compensate for scores below threshold in Technical Compliance or Cost** (see §4.4).

---

## 4. Scoring Model & Mechanics

### 4.1 Base Scale

| Score | Definition |
|-------|------------|
| 5     | Fully exceeds requirements with verifiable evidence and demonstrable added value |
| 4     | Fully meets requirements with strong, unambiguous evidence |
| 3     | Meets minimum acceptable threshold; some elements lack deep detail but are viable |
| 2     | Partial compliance with material gaps requiring remediation |
| 1     | Non-compliant or completely insufficient evidence provided |

### 4.2 Confidence Weighting

Every base score **MUST** be multiplied by a Confidence Score (0.0–1.0) at the **individual criterion/sub-criterion level** before any aggregation.

**Formula:** `Adjusted Score = Base Score × Confidence Score`

- **1.0 (High):** Backed by third-party audits, live demonstrations, or contractual guarantees.
- **0.75 (Medium):** Backed by strong internal case studies, detailed architecture diagrams, or referenceable clients.
- **0.50 (Low):** Relies on vendor marketing material, assertions, or vague future roadmap promises.

### 4.3 Aggregation Rules (New)

- **Sub-criteria within a category:** Equal weighting by default (unless explicitly declared otherwise in the category anchors).
- **Category Score:** Simple average of all adjusted sub-criterion scores (rounded to 2 decimal places).
- **Final Weighted Score:** Sum of (Category Score × Category Weight). Result normalised to 100-point scale.
- **Missing Criteria:** Treated as Base Score = 0 for calculation purposes.

**Worked Example** (simplified):
- Technical Compliance: 3 sub-criteria with adjusted scores 4.0, 4.5, 3.5 → Category Score = 4.0
- Final calculation follows standard weighted sum.

### 4.4 Innovation Uplift Cap

Innovation contribution is capped such that it cannot raise any vendor with a Technical Compliance or Cost category score below 3.0 above the disqualification threshold.

## 5. Hard Gating Rules (P0 Fail Conditions)

Automatic disqualification if **any** of the following apply:

- Failure to explicitly meet and verify 100% of P0 baseline requirements.
- **Material or statutory regulatory non-compliance** (distinguished from minor/procedural gaps scored under Risk & Compliance).
- Inability to support explicitly defined critical integration endpoints.
- Refusal to accept minimum baseline liability/risk allocations.

---

## 6. Expanded Calibration Anchors

### 6.1 Technical Compliance Anchors

- **5:** 100% P0/P1 coverage + fully populated traceability matrix + live architectural proof.
- **4:** ≥95% coverage with minor, non-critical gaps + solid design documentation.
- **3:** ≥90% coverage, acceptable deviations formally documented.
- **2:** <90% coverage or missing key P1 requirements.
- **1:** <75% coverage or critical P0 failure.

### 6.2 Cost & TCO Anchors

- **5:** >15% below benchmark TCO, transparent pricing model, favorable payment terms.
- **4:** 5-15% below benchmark TCO, clear OPEX predictability.
- **3:** Meets benchmark TCO limits, standard payment terms.
- **2:** Exceeds TCO benchmark by <10%, or contains hidden/variable scaling costs.
- **1:** Exceeds TCO benchmark by >10% or opaque pricing structure.

### 6.3 Delivery & Experience Anchors (New)

- **5:** Multiple verifiable similar-scale deployments with superior on-time/on-budget performance + strong references.
- **4:** Proven track record on comparable projects with ≥90% on-time delivery.
- **3:** Adequate relevant experience with acceptable timeline realism.
- **2:** Limited or partially relevant experience; timelines optimistic.
- **1:** No relevant experience or unrealistic delivery commitments.

### 6.4 Risk & Compliance Anchors (New)

- **5:** Exceptional risk mitigation, full regulatory compliance with proactive measures.
- **4:** Strong compliance posture with minor remediable gaps.
- **3:** Meets baseline compliance with acceptable residual risk.
- **2:** Material gaps in compliance or risk controls.
- **1:** Significant statutory or contractual risk exposure.

### 6.5 Innovation Anchors (New)

- **5:** Transformative capabilities with clear, evidence-based business value beyond RFQ-0000.
- **4:** Significant extensible features with strong implementation path.
- **3:** Moderate value-adding features.
- **2:** Minor or speculative additions.
- **1:** No meaningful innovation or rebadged baseline features.

## 7. Evaluation Workflow & Audit

1. Independent Scoring by evaluators.
2. Traceability Mapping to RTM line items.
3. Confidence Adjustment at criterion level.
4. **Variance Detection:** Absolute difference >1.0 (on base 1–5 scale, pre-confidence) between any two evaluators on the same criterion triggers mandatory review.
5. Consensus Moderation.
6. Final Aggregation per §4.3.

**Tie-Breaking Protocol (New):** In case of equal final weighted scores:
1. Highest Technical Compliance score.
2. Highest Cost score.
3. Estate Principal adjudication.

### 7.1 Audit Artefacts

**Vendor Submission Requirements:**
- Completed response template per vendor_response_schema
- Requirement Traceability Matrix (RTM)
- Evidence artefacts (diagrams, case studies, audits, references)

**Evaluator Artefact Requirements:**
- Per-criterion base scores + confidence justification
- Category and final weighted scores
- Variance reports and moderation notes
