# RFQ-0003: Meridian Evaluation Rubric & Scoring Standard (Revised v1.1)

**Version 1.1 — Consolidated Revision (2026-06-19)**

---

## 1. Purpose

This document defines a deterministic, auditable scoring model for vendor evaluation under the Meridian RFQ framework. It resolves ambiguity, removes category overlap, and introduces confidence-weighted scoring and hard gating rules.

---

## 2. Scope

Applies to all Meridian procurement evaluations including L2A/L2B automated scoring pipelines and human moderation layers.

---

## 3. Evaluation Architecture

| Category | Weight | Definition |
|----------|--------|------------|
| Technical Compliance | 35% | Conformance to explicit RFQ requirements (P0/P1/P2 mapped) |
| Cost | 30% | Total cost of ownership, lifecycle cost, financial exposure |
| Delivery & Experience | 15% | Proven execution capability and timeline realism |
| Innovation (Optional Uplift Only) | 10% | Non-required enhancements beyond baseline specification |
| Risk & Compliance | 10% | Regulatory adherence and operational risk profile |

**Constraint:** Innovation cannot include any requirement explicitly or implicitly specified in RFQ-0000.

---

## 4. Scoring Model

### 4.1 Base Scale

| Score | Definition |
|------|------------|
| 5 | Fully exceeds requirements with verifiable evidence |
| 4 | Fully meets requirements with strong evidence |
| 3 | Meets minimum acceptable threshold |
| 2 | Partial compliance with material gaps |
| 1 | Non-compliant or insufficient evidence |

---

### 4.2 Calibration Anchors (Mandatory)

Each sub-criterion MUST include explicit measurable anchors:

Example — Requirement Coverage:
- 5 = 100% P0/P1 coverage + full traceability matrix
- 4 = ≥95% coverage with minor non-critical gaps
- 3 = ≥90% coverage, acceptable deviations documented
- 2 = <90% coverage or missing key P1 requirements
- 1 = <75% coverage or critical failure

---

### 4.3 Confidence Weighting

Each score MUST include:

- Confidence score (0.0–1.0)

Final adjusted score = BaseScore × Confidence

This reduces inflation from weak or unverified vendor claims.

---

## 5. Hard Gating Rules (P0 Fail Conditions)

Automatic disqualification if any apply:

- Failure to meet P0 requirements
- Regulatory non-compliance
- Inability to meet baseline operational constraints
- Unsupported critical integrations

---

## 6. Category Breakdown

### 6.1 Technical Compliance (35%)

- Requirement Coverage (P0/P1/P2 mapping)
- Verification & Testability
- Integration Design Quality
- Technical Risk Mitigation

### 6.2 Cost (30%)

- Price Competitiveness vs benchmark
- Lifecycle Cost (TCO, OPEX, support)
- Payment Structure Risk Profile

### 6.3 Delivery & Experience (15%)

- Comparable project history
- Timeline feasibility
- Team capability & availability

### 6.4 Innovation (10%)

- Optional enhancements only
- Future extensibility not required by RFQ

### 6.5 Risk & Compliance (10%)

- Regulatory adherence
- Operational risk depth
- Contractual exposure

---

## 7. Evaluation Mechanics

1. Independent evaluator scoring
2. Requirement-to-score traceability mapping (mandatory)
3. Confidence-weighted aggregation
4. Inter-rater variance detection (>1.0 triggers review)
5. Consensus moderation layer
6. Final weighted score generation

---

## 8. Audit Requirements

All submissions must include:

- Requirement traceability matrix (RTM)
- Score justification per criterion
- Confidence scores per evaluation cell
- Variance report across evaluators

- Changes must be logged with rationale and impact assessment
